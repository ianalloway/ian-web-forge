# GEA #2 Video Script
## BSIS Reflective Essay (2-3 minutes)
### Tips: Speak naturally, don't read word-for-word. Use this as a guide.

---

## INTRO (~20 seconds)

Hi, my name is Ian Alloway. I'm a graduate of the BSIS program at USF with a concentration in Data Science and Analytics. I'm currently finishing my Master's in Artificial Intelligence here at USF, and today I want to share my reflections on the Information Science program and how it shaped my career path.

---

## MAIN CONTENT

### What I Gained (~40 seconds)

The BSIS program gave me a solid foundation across the information science landscape, from data management and analytics to understanding how information systems operate in real organizations. What I appreciated most was the breadth. I came in thinking information science was one thing, and the program opened my eyes to how many directions you can take it, whether that's data science, cybersecurity, UX, or systems design.

The data science concentration in particular gave me practical skills I use every day. Courses in data mining, predictive modeling, and database design gave me the technical toolkit I needed. I've since built and deployed multiple production-level machine learning projects, including an NBA sports betting prediction pipeline and several AI agent frameworks, and the analytical thinking I developed in the BSIS program is the backbone of all of that work.

### What I Liked and What Could Improve (~40 seconds)

What I liked most was the applied nature of the coursework. Projects felt relevant to real industry problems, not just academic exercises. The capstone and portfolio requirement, for example, pushed me to consolidate my work into something I could actually show employers, which has been valuable in my job search.

If I had to suggest improvements, I'd say the program could benefit from more exposure to modern AI and machine learning tools earlier in the curriculum. The field is moving fast, and students would benefit from hands-on experience with frameworks like Python-based ML libraries, cloud platforms, and API-driven development sooner rather than later. I'd also love to see more elective options around emerging areas like natural language processing and autonomous systems, since that's where so much of the industry is headed.

### The Capstone and Portfolio (~20 seconds)

The capstone experience was a good exercise in synthesis. It forced me to look back at everything I'd learned and present it as a cohesive body of work. My portfolio lives at ianalloway.xyz, and building it gave me a professional asset that goes beyond a resume. I think requiring the portfolio is one of the program's strengths, because it teaches students to communicate their skills in a way that actually matters in the job market.

---

## CONCLUSION (~20 seconds)

Overall, the BSIS program prepared me well for a career in data science and AI. It gave me the analytical foundation, the technical skills, and the professional communication abilities I needed to move into a graduate program and start building real products. I'm grateful for the experience, and I think with some updates to keep pace with the AI revolution, the program will continue to produce strong information professionals. Thank you.
